How a variable gets in the admission machinery the rest of this page assumes

1

The time-machine test

Every candidate faces the same trial: go back to six past tournaments (World Cups and Euros), rebuild the forecast using only what was known before each one, and ask, would this variable have predicted them better? If out-of-sample log-loss doesn't drop, it stays out.

2

The away leg

Passing at home isn't enough. A second panel, Copa América, AFCON, Asian and Gold Cups, can't admit anything (no squad files exist there) but it can veto: if a variable only works in Europe, it's a bias, not a signal. This veto is not theoretical, it caught football capital, whose "predictive power" turned out to be Europe-only coverage, and got it removed.

3

The verdict

Four outcomes. Admitted: in the official forecast (altitude). Probation: admitted on thin evidence, first in line for demotion (fatigue). Shadow: kept out of the forecast but scored live in the lab below, so the data keeps judging it in public (capital, cohesion, diaspora). Rejected: measured, failed, documented (heat, penalty bonus, the FIFA ranking).

What earned each team its odds the pre-tournament decomposition: quality vs the draw vs venue & freshness

A snapshot of the model's eve-of-tournament reasoning, kept as it was: with the group stage decided you can read it as a scorecard of why it rated each team the way it did.

What each bar measures: a team's pre-tournament chance of finishing top two of its group, qualifying straight to the knockouts (the numbers inside the bar are percentage points that add up to that total).

How we split it: we re-run the 20,000 tournaments three ways and read off the gaps - squad quality is how the team would do in an average group (its Elo-driven strength, the draw set aside); the draw is how much its actual group is easier than average; venue & freshness is what the model's only two earned adjustments - altitude and fatigue, add or take away. A drag (the hatched piece on the right) is anything working against the team, a tougher-than-average group or tired legs, so it's drawn past the total, not added to it. The three positive pieces sum exactly to the team's real number. Pre-tournament forecast.

The trials: admitted or cut every variable, measured by re-running the tournament without it

In one line: the model is Elo ratings → goals, plus exactly two earned adjustments, altitude and fatigue. Everything else we tried was cut: football capital, heat/climate, a penalty-taker bonus, plus diaspora and squad cohesion (kept only as "shadow bets", never in the real forecast). Also off the pitch

Penalty-taker bonus, rejected: it double-counts penalties already inside each scorer's history. Football capital (where players' clubs rank), its predictive power turned out to be a Europe-only geographic bias, so it was removed even though that cost a sliver of accuracy. Diaspora and squad cohesion can't (or barely) be backtested, they live as shadow bets in the model lab, never in the official forecast.

The methodology, in short for readers who want the formulas, not the metaphors

Two properties the cards above can't show. Every rating is point-in-time: a prediction for any date uses only matches played before it, so the backtests can't cheat. And the goal model has no per-team free parameters, five numbers shared by all 48 teams, which is why it can't overfit a single nation's story. Pre-registered backtest on World Cup 2022: log-loss 1.060 vs 1.099 for a know-nothing guess; favourites priced at 54% won 56%.
