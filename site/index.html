Who wins the
2026 World Cup?

An open-source forecast, built with AI from public data: all 104 matches simulated 20,000 times, updated after every match and scored against reality every game.

Under the hood: a proprietary Elo rating for every nation feeds a Dixon-Coles goal model, with two earned adjustments for altitude and fatigue. The ratings are frozen on the eve of the tournament (11 June).

The forecast

Every frame is a full re-simulation conditioned on the results up to that point (ratings and parameters set before kickoff). Step to the end for today's live picture, or back to watch one result ripple through everything. The control repeats above each view so you never have to scroll back up, use the ◀ ▶ buttons or arrow keys to move one match at a time. ▲/▼ = change since the previous match.

Round by round, chance of reaching each stage

% = share of the 20,000 simulations in which the team reaches that round; the last column is the title odds. Scroll for every team, it all moves with the slider, through to the final.

Model vs market our title odds against the bookies, live, with their built-in margin stripped out

Drag to watch our title odds move match by match (▲▼ = change since the tournament started); the market line is the latest live price. The bookmakers' odds always add up to more than 100% (their built-in profit margin); we strip that out so the two bars are a fair like-for-like. Scroll for more teams.
